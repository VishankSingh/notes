A prior $p(\boldsymbol{\theta}) \in \mathcal{F}$ is a conjugate prior for a likelihood function $p(\boldsymbol{y}|\boldsymbol{\theta})$ if the posterior $p(\boldsymbol{\theta}|\boldsymbol{y})$ is in the same family $\mathcal{F}$ as the prior. In other words, if the prior and likelihood are conjugate, then the posterior is also in the same family.
To note, if the family $\mathcal{F}$ corresponds to the [[Exponential Family]] of distributions, then the computation of the posterior is particularly straightforward, achieving a closed-form solution.
