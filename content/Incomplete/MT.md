<span class="blue"><strong>Definition</strong> (<em>$\color{#338cc7}{\sigma}$-algebra</em>):</span>
Let $E$ be a set and $\mathcal{E}$ a family of subset of $E$.
Then, $\mathcal{E}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra (resp. an algebra) if,

- $E \in \mathcal{E}$
- $\mathcal{E}$ is stable w.r.t. set complementation
- $\mathcal{E}$ is stable w.r.t. countable (resp. finite) unions.
